# Code of Conduct

## Our Pledge

We as members of this community are committed to providing a friendly, welcoming, and inclusive environment for all contributors, and making a very clear effort to make sure we make this happen.

As contributors and maintainers, we pledge to:
- Make personalized efforts to communicate kindly, respectfully, and honourably.
- Be mindful of biases and strive to create an inclusive environment.
- Be quick to point out problematic behaviour.
- Be mindful of the boundaries between professional and personal interaction.

## Our Standards

Example of behavior we consider acceptable:
- Using welcoming and inclusive language.
- Being respectful of differing opinions, viewpoints, and experiences.
- Gracefully accepting constructive criticism.
- Focusing on the actual problem rather than the person.
- Showing empathy and understanding.

Example of behavior we consider unacceptable:
- The use of stereotyped gender, racial, or sexual slurs.
- Trolling, insulting, or harassing specific community members.
- Persistent disruption of talks or other community events.
- Publicly shaming or mocking other community members.
- Using sexualized language or imagery.

## Enforcement

Instances of abusive behavior including but not limited to harassment, doxing, stalking, posting needless warnings, and other forms of harassment will not be tolerated.

If you see something that is not okay, please report it to the maintainer:
- **Nguyễn Thanh Phương** via [t.me/phuonguno](https://t.me/phuonguno) or GitHub.

## Scope

This Code of Conduct applies within all project spaces, including repositories, issue trackers, forums, social media, and in-person events.
